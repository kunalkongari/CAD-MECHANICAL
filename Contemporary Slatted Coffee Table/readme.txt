Project: Contemporary Slatted Coffee Table
Software Used
PTC Creo Parametric: Utilized for high-fidelity wood grain texturing, glass transparency, and top-down assembly.

Purpose / Problem Solved
In modern interior design, furniture must balance structural stability with visual lightness. This Contemporary Coffee Table solves the "bulkiness" of traditional wooden furniture by using a cantilever-style pillar design and a transparent glass top, creating an airy feel while maintaining the warmth of natural wood textures.

Key Technical Actions
Linear Patterning: Engineered a lower storage shelf using parametric linear patterns to ensure uniform slat spacing and easy scalability.

Surface Constraints: Applied Tangent and Coincident mates to "nest" the glass top into the pillar notches, simulating a real-world friction-fit assembly.

Aesthetic Rendering: Optimized lighting and material reflectivity (Glossy Wood vs. Transparent Glass) to create a client-ready visual presentation.

Notched Joinery: Designed the four main pillars with recessed shoulders to physically lock the glass top in place, a key safety feature.

Materials & Design Decisions
Frame: Dark Walnut or Mahogany texture for a premium, heavy-set look.

Top: 10mm Tempered Glass for durability and modern appeal.

Shelf: Slatted wooden design to allow for airflow and prevent dust accumulation.

Project Results
Visual Fidelity: Achieved a professional-grade render that demonstrates a mastery of CAD appearances and environment lighting.

Modular Logic: Created a 20+ piece assembly where the slat count can be updated via a single parameter.

Design Balance: Validated the load-bearing pillar geometry to ensure equal weight distribution across the glass surface.
