Project: Dual-Shaft Industrial Shredder
Software Used
PTC Creo Parametric: Used for 50+ part assembly, high-torque gear design, and sheet metal hopper fabrication.

Purpose / Problem Solved
Industrial waste management requires high-torque machinery capable of tearing through dense materials (plastics, tires, or wood). This Dual-Shaft Shredder provides a synchronized, counter-rotating cutting system that pulls material into the "crush zone," preventing the jams often seen in single-shaft or gravity-only designs.

Key Technical Actions
Hexagonal Torque Drive: Modeled specialized hex-profile shafts to maximize torque transmission and prevent cutting blades from slipping under extreme loads.

Gear Train Synchronization: Designed a 1:1 spur gear drive to perfectly time the interlocking of the dual cutters.

Sheet Metal Design: Engineered a tapered, bolt-on hopper to guide material flow and a discharge chute for seamless material output.

Interlocking Geometry: Developed "hooked" blade profiles that facilitate a shear-and-tear action rather than a simple crushing force.

Materials & Design Decisions
Blades: Hardened Alloy Steel (specified for high wear resistance).

Housing: Heavy-duty reinforced plates with bolt-together access for maintenance.

Shafts: High-tensile steel to resist torsional fatigue during the shredding cycle.

Project Results
Mechanical Validation: Successfully simulated the counter-rotation of the blade assembly through gear-driven kinematic constraints.

Industrial Scalability: Produced a manufacturing-ready model featuring standard fastener sizes and modular blade replacement.

Branding Integration: Utilized 3D text features for "Kunal Industries" branding, demonstrating attention to corporate product identity.
