Project: Modular Arch-Roof Shed System
Software Used
PTC Creo Parametric: Employed for large-scale structural assembly, pattern-driven geometry, and reflection-based rendering.

Purpose / Problem Solved
Traditional fixed-size sheds lack the flexibility needed for varying industrial or agricultural sites. This Modular Shed solves the problem by using a "tongue-and-groove" style roofing logic where each arched section fits perfectly with the next. This allows a user to extend the length of the structure indefinitely simply by adding parallel segments.

Key Technical Actions
Interlocking Geometric Logic: Designed the roof profiles specifically to overlap and fit together parallely, ensuring a weather-tight seal without needing complex custom fasteners for every joint.

Parametric Patterning: Used Directional Patterns to replicate the support pillars and roof ribs, allowing the entire length of the shed to be updated by changing a single "Quantity" variable.

Skeletal Structure: Engineered an internal ribbing system (visible in the under-arch view) to provide high structural rigidity while minimizing total material weight.

Surface Modeling: Utilized advanced sweeps to create the arched roof transitions, including the integrated side-gutter wings for water runoff management.

Materials & Design Decisions
Panels: Corrugated Galvanized Steel or Polycarbonate (simulated with a metallic grey finish) for weather resistance.

Support Pillars: Heavy-duty hollow section steel with reinforced base plates for bolting to concrete foundations.

Modular Width: The arch is designed at a fixed radius to optimize the strength-to-weight ratio, allowing for maximum interior clearance height.

Project Results
Scalable Assembly: Successfully created a master assembly that can be "stretched" for different site requirements without redesigning individual parts.

Structural Efficiency: Validated the rib-and-pillar alignment to ensure load paths are transferred vertically to the foundation plates.

Industrial Utility: Developed a design that prioritizes ease of assembly, making it suitable for rapid-deployment storage or temporary housing.
