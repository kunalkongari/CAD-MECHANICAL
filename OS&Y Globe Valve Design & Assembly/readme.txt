Project: OS&Y Globe Valve Design & Assembly
Software Used
PTC Creo Parametric: Primary tool for 3D parametric modeling, assembly management, and technical cross-sections.

Purpose / Problem Solved
Standard gate valves are poor at regulating flow because they suffer from "wire-drawing" (erosion) when partially open. This Globe Valve solves that by using a perpendicular disc-to-seat movement, providing a precise throttling capability for steam, oil, and gas applications where flow control is as important as shut-off.

Key Technical Actions
Complex Internal Geometry: Modeled the "S-shaped" flow path within the valve body to ensure consistent wall thickness for pressure containment.

Outside Screw & Yoke (OS&Y): Designed an external threading system to isolate the stem threads from the process fluid, preventing corrosion and extending service life.

Mechanical Constraints: Applied Screw and Cylindrical mates to synchronize handwheel rotation with linear stem travel.

Sectional Validation: Used 2D cross-sections to verify the "bubble-tight" seal between the plug and the seat ring.

Materials & Design Decisions
Body/Bonnet: Cast Carbon Steel for high-pressure durability.

Stem: Stainless Steel for smooth, non-corrosive thread action.

Handwheel: Ductile Iron (Blue) for ergonomic manual operation.

Gland Packing: Dual-stud compression system to ensure a zero-leak seal around the moving stem.

Project Results
High-Fidelity Assembly: Successfully integrated 15+ unique components into a fully constrained, motion-ready CAD model.

Design for Maintenance (DFM): Produced a detailed Exploded View to document the assembly/disassembly sequence for field technicians.

Functional Accuracy: Validated the full stroke of the valve, ensuring the disc clears the flow path entirely when open and seats perfectly when closed.
